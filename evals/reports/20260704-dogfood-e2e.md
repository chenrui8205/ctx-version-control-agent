## Verdict in one paragraph

**The write-path safety net and the read-path hand-off both work — and the run found one
data-loss-class defect that must be fixed before M1.** Every planted high-stakes case passed live:
the duplicate dropped, the R06 change-language collision superseded cleanly with no review noise, the
stale both-current assertion fired `contradicts`, froze master, and resolved correctly, and the final
open-threads set was exactly right. The hand-off exam was won by the wiki agent (12/12 vs 11.5/12) on
22% less context, with the margin coming precisely from what the platform uniquely records (conflict
resolution provenance). But **cross-type supersede over-firing silently ate 4 entries during the week
— including both team decisions pushed alongside their question-closes** — an unlabeled-drop failure
mode strictly worse than the labeled false-drop the gates watch for.

## Defect ledger (all evidence preserved; nothing fixed mid-run)

- **DF-1 — same-target double-supersede collapse (4 events; S2, S3, S6×2).** Two incoming entries both
  classified refines/subsumes against one master target; apply_actions lets the second overwrite the
  first under the adopted entry_id. Casualties: Sam's UI state_change; **Riley's cent-allocation
  decision**; Riley's deploy state_change; **the receipts decision**. 2 of 2 decisions pushed together
  with their question-closes were lost. Root: no batch guard + classifier over-firing (below).
- **DF-2 — cross-type supersede retired open work (S3).** A state_change (whose body *said* the step
  stays open) superseded Alex's open next_step; it vanished from open-threads. Detected by a human
  only once (S6 Riley: noticed, shrugged, moved on — exactly how this hides in real teams).
- **DF-3 — default proposed resolution is dangerous (S4).** `{"winner": "incoming"}` (spec §6 default)
  would have laundered the stale 50 over Riley's reasoned 20 on a lazy one-click.
- **DF-4 — type mutation under a stable entry_id (S5).** A finding subsumed a closed open_question;
  the entry changed type on master. Also cascade evidence: the classifier picked the closed question
  because DF-1b had already eaten the decision that should have been the anchor.

**Single root cause across all four:** the "same subject + newer ⇒ refines" prior over-fires on
**cross-type pairs**, and nothing downstream (routing policy or transaction) checks type compatibility
or same-target multiplicity. All 6 action misses in the run are this one pattern.

Both agents independently flagged the missing settle-up step (DF-2's damage) as an inconsistency —
the journal-vs-open-threads cross-check works even on a corrupted state.

**Threats to validity, stated honestly:** (1) the synthetic transcripts are far more disciplined than
real session logs — the baseline is inflated; (2) at 6 sessions the corpora are similar sizes; the
wiki's advantage compounds with time (transcripts grow linearly, the wiki stays O(current state));
(3) one consistency artifact (JSX in a "vanilla JS" project in s5) shows simulated devs drift — real
dogfooding remains irreplaceable, which is what M1 is for.

## UX asset log (verbatim themes from the 8 agents; M1 backlog candidates)

1. Preview should state **consequences**, not just actions ("this will remove X from open-threads") — S3.
2. Dropped entries deserve a loud receipt with a link to what already covers them — S2.
3. Per-conflict machine proposal must weigh change-language + commit recency, or offer no default — S4.
4. `threads --closed-since <commit>` diff view (observability gap that let DF-2 hide) — S6.
5. Registry needs `--describe` (one-liner per subject) for cheap reuse decisions under time pressure — S5.
6. Preview rows should echo entry gists (correlating temp_ids by position is fragile) — S1.
7. Conflict card should show the existing side's commit age — S4. Job result should embed conflict_id + bodies — S4.
8. Skill gaps: origin rule for agent-executed-human-directed changes; decision+close both-push convention; raw REST read paths documented — S5/S6/S2.
9. Deterministic fast-path when master is empty / subject brand-new (9 needless LLM calls in S1) — S1.
10. CLI: inject `subject` into fields; client-side schema prevalidation; poll-hint in stage response — S4/S2/S5.

## Recommended fixes (FOR DISCUSSION — none applied)

1. **Pipeline guard (deterministic):** at most one supersede per target entry_id per batch; further
   supersedes of the same target downgrade to keep + flag in preview. Invariant test, not fixture.
2. **Routing policy (deterministic):** cross-type supersede is invalid — auto-downgrade to
   complementary/keep. Same-type supersede (incl. status closes) unaffected. Kills DF-1/2/4 at the
   routing table even when the classifier errs.
3. **Prompt:** add cross-type guidance to `reconcile.md`; rerun the live eval on the 25-pair set
   (now includes R22–R25) and commit the report.
4. **DF-3:** replace the hardcoded `winner: incoming` proposal with change-language/recency-aware
   heuristic, or no default.
5. Triage the UX log into M1 scope (items 1–4 are cheap and high-value).
